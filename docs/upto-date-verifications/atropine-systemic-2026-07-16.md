## Brand Names (US)

## Therapeutic Category
Antiasthmatic; Anticholinergic Agent; Antidote, Organophosphate Poisoning; Antispasmodic Agent, GI; Bronchodilator

---

## DOSING: NEONATAL

### Bradycardia
**NOTE:** Neonatal resuscitation guidelines do NOT include atropine for treatment of bradycardia.

| Route | Dose | Repeat |
|---|---|---|
| IV / IO | 0.02 mg/kg/dose | May repeat once in 3-5 min; reserve for patients unresponsive to improved oxygenation/ventilation and epinephrine |
| Endotracheal | 0.04-0.06 mg/kg/dose | May repeat once if needed; immediately follow with 1 mL NS |

⚠️ Use of minimum dose of 0.1 mg in patients <5 kg results in doses >0.02 mg/kg → toxicity and death reported.

### Intubation, premedication (preferred vagolytic)
IV, IM: 0.02 mg/kg/dose — **a minimum dose of 0.1 mg/dose is NOT recommended**

### Organophosphate / carbamate / nerve agent poisoning
**IV, IM, IO Initial:** 0.05-0.1 mg/kg; repeat q3-5 min, double dose if previous did not induce atropinization. Maintain atropinization 2-12 hr PRN.

**Continuous IV infusion:** 10-20% of total loading dose per hour, titrated to maintain atropinization without toxicity.

**Endotracheal:** Increase IV dose 2-3×; mix in 3-5 mL NS, flush with 3-5 mL NS, follow with 5 assisted manual ventilations.

## DOSING: PEDIATRIC (Infants, Children, Adolescents)

### 1. Bradycardia
| Route | Dose | Min | Max | Repeat |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| IV / IO | 0.02 mg/kg/dose | 0.1 mg/dose | 0.5 mg/dose | May repeat once in 5 min; reserve for patients unresponsive to improved oxygenation + epinephrine |
| Endotracheal | 0.04-0.06 mg/kg/dose | — | — | May repeat once if needed |

⚠️ Some have suggested the 0.1 mg minimum should NOT be used in patients <5 kg.

### 2. Inhibit salivation and secretions (preoperative/intraoperative)
| Age | Route | Dose | Max single | Repeat | Max total |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| <12 years | IM/IV/SUBQ | 0.02 mg/kg/dose | 0.5 mg/dose | q4-6 hr PRN | 1 mg/procedure |
| ≥12 years + adolescents | IM/IV/SUBQ | 0.02 mg/kg/dose | 1 mg/dose | q4-6 hr PRN | 2 mg/procedure |

First dose 30-60 min preoperatively.

### 3. Intubation, emergent (premedication)
**Note:** Routine use NOT recommended for preintubation in infants/children. May be considered for high-risk bradycardia situations (eg, succinylcholine, septic shock).

IV, IO: Usual 0.02 mg/kg/dose **with no minimum dose**; range 0.01-0.02 mg/kg/dose; max 0.5 mg/dose.

### 4. Muscarine-containing mushroom poisoning (limited data)
IV: 0.02 mg/kg/dose; min 0.1 mg. Titrate and repeat as needed to reverse symptoms (ie, titrate to decreased bronchial secretions).

### 5. Organophosphate / carbamate / nerve agent poisoning

**IV, IM, IO:**
| Age | Initial | Repeat | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Infants & Children | 0.05-0.1 mg/kg | q3-5 min, double if no atropinization | Maintain ≥2-12 hr PRN |
| Adolescents | 1-3 mg | q3-5 min, double if no atropinization | Maintain ≥2-12 hr PRN |

**Continuous IV infusion (all ages):** 10-20% of total loading dose per hour titrated to maintain atropinization.

**IM (AtroPen) weight-directed dosing:**
| Weight | Dose | Pen color |
|---|---|---|
| <7 kg (<15 lb) | 0.25 mg | Yellow |
| 7-18 kg (15-40 lb) | 0.5 mg | Blue |
| >18-41 kg (>40-90 lb) | 1 mg | Dark red |
| >41 kg (>90 lb) | 2 mg | Green |

- **Mild symptoms (≥2):** One weight-directed dose ASAP. If severe symptoms develop, 2 additional doses in rapid succession 10 min later. **Do not exceed 3 doses.**
- **Severe symptoms (≥1):** Immediately three weight-directed doses in rapid succession.

**Endotracheal:** Increase IV dose 2-3×. Mix in 3-5 mL NS, flush with 3-5 mL NS, follow with 5 assisted manual ventilations.

## ADVERSE REACTIONS (frequency not always defined; dose-related)
- **Cardiovascular:** Asystole, atrial arrhythmia, atrial fibrillation, AV dissociation (transient), bigeminy, bradycardia, chest pain, hypotension, ECG changes (multiple), ectopic beats, flushing
- **CNS:** Agitation (children), amnesia, anxiety, ataxia, behavioral changes, coma, confusion, delirium, dizziness, drowsiness, hallucination, headache, hyperpyrexia, hyperreflexia, insomnia, irritability (children)
- **Dermatologic:** Anhidrosis, dry/hot skin, rash
- **Endocrine/Metabolic:** Dehydration, hyperglycemia, hypoglycemia, hypokalemia, hyponatremia
- **GI:** Constipation, dry mucous membranes, nausea, paralytic ileus, vomiting, xerostomia
- **GU:** Difficulty in micturition, urinary retention
- **Hematologic:** Leukocytosis, petechiae
- **Ophthalmic:** Angle-closure glaucoma (acute), blurred vision, decreased accommodation, dry eye, mydriasis, photophobia
- **Respiratory:** Bradypnea, dyspnea, pulmonary edema, respiratory failure, stridor
- **Misc:** Fever, swelling (children)

## CONTRAINDICATIONS
**US labeling: None listed.**

**Canadian labeling:**
- Hypersensitivity to atropine or any component
- GI obstruction
- Glaucoma (known or suspected)
- Pyloric stenosis or prostatic hypertrophy (except in doses typically used for preanesthesia)

## WARNINGS / PRECAUTIONS

**Concerns related to adverse effects:**
- **Hypersensitivity:** Anaphylactic reactions may occur.
- **Hyperthermia:** May inhibit sweating → heat-related injury/hyperthermia in warm environments or exercise.
- **Psychosis:** Can occur in sensitive individuals or with excessive doses.

**Disease-related concerns:**
- **Arrhythmias:** Avoid for type II 2nd-degree or 3rd-degree AV block. Asystole/bradycardic PEA: routine use no longer recommended (ACLS 2010).
- **Cardiovascular disease:** Caution in myocardial ischemia, heart failure, tachyarrhythmias, hypertension.
- **Chronic lung disease:** May thicken bronchial secretions → dangerous viscid plugs.
- **Glaucoma:** Caution in severe narrow-angle; may precipitate acute glaucoma.
- **Hepatic impairment:** Effects may be prolonged in severe hepatic impairment.
- **Hiatal hernia:** Caution with reflux esophagitis.
- **Hyperthyroidism:** Use with caution.
- **Myasthenia gravis:** Extreme caution; may precipitate myasthenic crisis.
- **Neuropathy:** Caution in autonomic neuropathy.
- **Pyloric stenosis:** Caution in partial stenosis; may cause complete obstruction.
- **Renal impairment:** Effects may be prolonged in severe renal impairment.
- **Urinary retention:** Caution in urinary obstruction.

**Special populations:**
- **Heart transplant recipients:** Likely ineffective (lack of vagal innervation). Paradoxical slowing/high-degree AV block may occur (ACLS 2010).
- **Pediatric:** Children more sensitive to anticholinergic effects. Caution in spastic paralysis.

**Dosage form specific:**
- **Benzyl alcohol:** Some dosage forms contain benzyl alcohol/benzoate. Large amounts (≥99 mg/kg/day) associated with "gasping syndrome" in neonates (metabolic acidosis, respiratory distress, gasping, CNS dysfunction, hypotension, cardiovascular collapse).

**Other:** Anticholinesterase poisoning: Refer to Lexidrug Toxicology: Organophosphates.

## WARNINGS: ADDITIONAL PEDIATRIC CONSIDERATIONS
**⚠️ CRITICAL:** Several reports describe neonates developing **central anticholinergic syndrome** after receiving atropine for bradycardia at doses of 0.1 mg (the minimum dose described to prevent paradoxical bradycardia). When evaluated by weight, this dose exceeded 0.02 mg/kg in all cases (Gillick 1974; Prakash 2017; Rizzi 2004). **Some literature recommends against using a minimum dose in patients <5 kg to prevent overdose** (Barrington 2011; Prakash 2017).

## ADMINISTRATION: PEDIATRIC

**Endotracheal:** May administer undiluted, followed by 1-5 mL NS flush, OR dilute in NS/sterile water (1-2 mg in ≤10 mL) and follow with 5 assisted manual ventilations. For cardiac arrest, stop compressions during drug admin, resume after manual ventilation.

**Flush volumes for ET:**
- Bradycardia neonates: ≥1 mL NS
- Bradycardia infants/children/adolescents: 5 mL NS
- Organophosphate: 3-5 mL NS

**Parenteral IV:** Administer undiluted by rapid IV injection. **Slow injection may result in paradoxical bradycardia.**

**Parenteral IM (AtroPen):** Administer to outer thigh. Remove safety release, jab at 90°. May be given through clothing (no pockets at site). Bunch thigh in thin patients or <6.8 kg.

## PREPARATION FOR ADMINISTRATION: PEDIATRIC
**Mass chemical terrorism (bulk prep):** Add atropine sulfate powder to 100 mL NS in PVC bags → final concentration 1 mg/mL.
