# Push-Dose Pressors in Pediatrics — Research Findings

**Decision (2026-07-16):** Drop phenylephrine from peds section. Keep only epinephrine. Tighten dose to 1 mcg/kg single value.

---

## Consensus dose for pediatric push-dose epinephrine

**1 mcg/kg IV/IO, max 10 mcg per dose, repeat every 2-3 minutes.**

### Source agreement

| Source | Dose | Max | Repeat | Evidence level | Population |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **AHA 2018 PALS scientific statement** (PMID: 29685887) | 1 μg/kg (1/10 arrest dose) | not explicit | titrate to effect | **Class IIa, LoE C** (expert opinion) | At-risk myocardium / pre-arrest |
| **Orange County EMS PR-205** (Oct 2024) | 0.1 mL/kg of 10 mcg/mL = **1 mcg/kg** | **10 mcg** | q3 min | Protocol | Prehospital peds, post-fluid |
| **NorCal EMS** (2021) | 0.1 mL/kg of 10 mcg/mL = **1 mcg/kg** | **10 mcg** | q1-5 min | Protocol | Prehospital peds |
| **PEMPlaybook** (2023) | 1 mcg/kg | not explicit | q1-2 min | Educational | ED/PICU |
| **REBEL EM "Epi Spritzer"** (Bryant 2018) | 1 mcg/kg | not explicit | q2 min | Educational | ED/PICU |

**All 5 sources converge on 1 mcg/kg.** No source found that recommends a different peds dose.

### Prep (universal)
- Take 1 mL of cardiac epinephrine (1:10,000 = 0.1 mg/mL = 100 mcg/mL)
- Waste the 1 mL
- Draw up 9 mL normal saline into the syringe
- Result: 10 mcg/mL
- Dose: **0.1 mL/kg** of this dilution

### SBP targets (from OC PR-205)
- <10 years: SBP > 70 + (age × 2)
- ≥10 years: SBP > 90

## Why phenylephrine was dropped

1. **No pediatric-specific evidence.** Every source I found for peds push-dose pressors is epi-only.
2. **AHA 2018 PALS scientific statement** does not mention phenylephrine for pre-arrest peds hypotension.
3. **The 2 authoritative pediatric EMS protocols** (OC PR-205, NorCal) both stock epi only for peds.
4. **PEMPlaybook + REBEL EM** both discuss epi only.
5. **Cole 2019 (J Med Toxicol, n=249 adults)** is the only large safety dataset:
   - 39% adverse hemodynamic events
   - 50% adverse events in epi group, 27% in phenylephrine group
   - 19% human error rate
   - 7/249 (3%) had 2.5- to 100-fold dosing errors
   - 43/249 (17%) documentation errors
   - **Only 1 dosing error when a pharmacist was present**
6. **The 100 mcg/mL concentration** used in the old app row is an adult OR convention. In a 10 kg kid, 0.5-5 mcg/kg = 5-50 mcg = 0.05-0.5 mL of a 100 mcg/mL solution — **micro-volumes prone to 10-fold error**.
7. **Pure α-agonist reflex bradycardia** risk in a child with potential shock — no outcome data to support this choice over epi.

## What the app now says

**Single row:**
- Epinephrine push-dose: **1 mcg/kg IV/IO, max 10 mcg/dose, q2-3 min**
- Concentration: 10 mcg/mL (prep above)
- SBP targets: <10y → 70+(age×2); ≥10y → 90
- Tagged with SRC_AHA_PALS_2018 and SRC_OC_EMS_PR205

**Section header note** in the note field flags it as LOW-EVIDENCE and points to Cole 2019 for the safety data.

## Gap to watch

- Cole 2019 is **adults only** (≥16 yr). No equivalent pediatric safety dataset.
- AHA 2018 statement is the only "guideline" — Class IIa, LoE C.
- REBEL EM (Bryant 2018) and PEMPlaybook are educational, not evidence.
- If a better source emerges, re-evaluate.
